Chef Aman Verma

Raised between the saffron fields of Pampore and the spice auctions of Fort Kochi, Chef Aman is devoted to India’s terroir. He sources from artisans cultivating small-batch fenugreek, dry-ages tandoor-roasted meats over black stone, and ferments jaggery to coax out hidden sweetness.

At Aurelia, every plate is a story: langoustines smoked over sandalwood, fermented jackfruit glazed with tamarind molasses, kulcha mille-feuille layered with goat butter. The kitchen breathes through clay, charcoal, and patience.

“Culinary poems in saffron light.”
